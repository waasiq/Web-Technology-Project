Heritage of Pakistan

Pakistan has a rich history dating back to 3300 BCE. One of the earliest forms of civilization found in Pakistan were of the Mohenjedaro and Harappa civilization. Pakistan's cultural heritage includes archaeological sites, stupas, forts, shrines, tombs, buildings, residences, monuments, and places of worship. There are total of 6 UNESCO heritage sites in Pakistan. Visited by millions of people every year these sites are well appreciated by foreigners and truly hold an amazement in themselves. UNESCO heritage sites in pakistan are listed as following:

  • Mohenjo-daro
  • Taxila
  • Rohtas Fort
  • Shalimar Bagh
  • Thatta
  • Takht-i-Bahi
  • Lahore Fort
  • Makli Necropolis

Sports of Pakistan

Sport in Pakistan is a significant part of Pakistani culture. Cricket is the most popular sport in Pakistan, while field hockey, polo, and squash are also popular. Traditional sports like kabaddi and other well-known games are also played. The Pakistan Sports Board was created in 1962 by the Ministry of Education as a corporate body for the purposes of promoting and developing uniform standards of competition in sports in Pakistan comparable to the standards prevailing internationally, and regulating and controlling sports in Pakistan on a national basis. The Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism, now has control over the Pakistan Sports Board. The PSB controls all 39 sporting federations. The Pakistan Sports Board is supported by the Pakistan Sports Trust, which assists hard up players and associations so they can continue participating in sports. Over recent years there has been an increase in sporting activity in Pakistan, with Pakistani sportsmen and women participating at many national and international events. Also, more international tournaments now take place in Pakistan. The size of the teams Pakistan sends, and the number of events they participate in, such as the Olympic Games, Asian Games, World Games, and Commonwealth Games has increased since the turn of the century.

Pakistan is rich in sports winning international world cups in Squash, Cricket, Snooker, Hockey, Kabaddi.
